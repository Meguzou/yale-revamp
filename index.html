History & Trivia

Historical Milestones

  • 1832: The Trumbull Gallery opens, one of the first art museums in the Anglo-Saxon world and the first connected with a college in America.
  • 1864: Yale Art School is established through the generosity of Augustus Russell Street.
  • 1869: Yale School of the Fine Arts opens as the first art school connected with an institution of higher learning in the country.
  • 1869: Becomes the first Yale school to admit women, who formed the majority of students in the School's first four decades.
  • 2000: School moves to renovated 1156 Chapel Street building, named after Holcombe T. Green, Jr.

Did You Know?

  • The school was founded by patriot-artist Colonel John Trumbull, one-time aide-de-camp to General Washington.
  • Painter-inventor Samuel F. B. Morse, a Yale College graduate, was one of the original art council members.
  • The school's co-educational policy was pioneering - it was established through Augustus and Caroline Street's bequest specifically requiring admission of both sexes.
  • The School of Art has been led by 14 deans since its founding, with Kymberly Pinder becoming the first woman of color to hold the position in 2021.
  • The school's website functions as a collaborative wiki where all community members can contribute content.

About This Website

This website exists as an ongoing collaborative experiment in digital publishing and information sharing. Because this website functions as a wiki, all members of the School of Art community—graduate students, faculty, staff, and alums—have the ability to add new content and pages, and to edit most of the site's existing content.
